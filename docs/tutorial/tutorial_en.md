# AI PEER: Patient Tutorial

Welcome. This short guide walks you through the basics of the AI PEER app: signing in, moving around the home screen, taking the questionnaire, completing a balance test, and starting an exercise session. Keep this tutorial with you for your first few sessions.

## 1. Open the app and sign in

1. Tap the **AI PEER** icon on your phone to open the app.
2. Enter your **phone number**. Then tap **Next**.
3. A text message with a 6-digit code will arrive on your phone.
4. Type that 6-digit code on the **Verify** screen. Then tap **Verify**.

*First-time users:* after you verify your code, you will see a short **Welcome** screen. It lets you choose your **text size**, your **display contrast** (light, dark, or high contrast), your **language** (English, Spanish, or Haitian Creole), and whether you want to hear **sound alerts**. Tap **Next** to move through each step, and **Finish** when you reach the end.

## 2. Your home screen

The home screen shows a small **Fall Risk Matrix** card and three large buttons:

- **Balance Test:** opens the two short clinical tests.
- **Questionnaire:** opens the 7-question fear-of-falling survey.
- **Let's Chat:** opens the AI helper (available on some phones).

The Fall Risk Matrix gives you a quick look at how your body and your own concerns about falling are doing. It updates as you use the app.

## 3. Take the questionnaire

1. On the home screen, tap **Questionnaire**.
2. Read the short list of instructions and tap **Begin Assessment**.
3. You will see 7 questions. For each one, tap how worried you would feel about falling during that activity: **Not at all concerned**, **Somewhat concerned**, **Fairly concerned**, or **Very concerned**.
4. After the final question, tap **View Results**.
5. Your score and a short explanation appear on the screen. Tap **Save Results** to keep them.

You can take the questionnaire again any time. Each time you do, the home screen Fall Risk Matrix updates.

## 4. Do a balance test

The app includes two short clinical tests:

- **Chair Rise:** how many times you can stand up and sit down in 30 seconds.
- **Timed Up and Go:** how long it takes you to stand up, walk to a marker, turn around, come back, and sit down.

To do the **Chair Rise** test:

1. On the home screen, tap **Balance Test**, then tap **Chair Rise**.
2. Place your phone where the camera can see your whole body. Sit in a sturdy chair with your arms crossed across your chest.
3. Tap **Start Test**. When the camera asks, raise your open palm toward it to begin.
4. A 5-second countdown will play.
5. Stand up fully and sit back down as many times as you can. The app counts each one out loud.
6. After 30 seconds, the test ends and your result appears on the screen.

The **Timed Up and Go** test works the same way. The app will tell you when to stand, when to walk to the marker, and when to sit back down.

**Safety first:** always use a sturdy chair and make sure the floor in front of you is clear. Stop and sit down right away if you feel unsteady.

## 5. Start an exercise session

1. Tap the **Exercise** tab at the bottom of the screen.
2. Pick a category: **Warm-Up**, **Strength**, or **Balance**.
3. Tap any exercise. The app shows a short demonstration video. When you are ready, tap **Start Monitoring**.
4. Place your phone so the camera can see your full body. Follow along with the exercise. The app counts your reps out loud and shows your form score on the screen.

### Tips for good tracking

- Stand about 6 to 8 feet away from your phone.
- Use good lighting so the camera can see you clearly.
- Make sure your full body is in the picture from head to feet.
